# COGNIFYZ-Descriptive-Analysis
# 🍽️ Customer Preference Analysis

This task focuses on analyzing customer preferences based on restaurant cuisines, ratings, and votes.

---

## 📌 Objective

The objective of this analysis is to:
- Understand the relationship between cuisines and restaurant ratings  
- Identify the most popular cuisines based on customer votes  
- Determine which cuisines tend to receive higher ratings  

---

## 📂 Dataset Features Used

The following columns were used:

- Cuisines  
- Aggregate Rating  
- Votes  

---

## 🔍 Analysis Performed

### 1. Cuisine vs Rating

- Calculated the average rating for each cuisine  
- Sorted cuisines based on highest ratings  

👉 This helps identify which cuisines are rated highly by customers.

---

### 2. Popular Cuisines (Based on Votes)

- Aggregated total votes for each cuisine  
- Sorted cuisines by highest votes  

👉 This helps identify the most preferred cuisines among customers.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Some cuisines have very high average ratings (close to 4.9), but they may not be widely popular due to fewer restaurants or votes.  

- Popular cuisines such as North Indian and Chinese receive a high number of votes, indicating strong customer demand.  

- There is a clear difference between **highly rated cuisines** and **most popular cuisines**, showing that popularity and quality perception may vary.

---

## 💡 Conclusion

The analysis shows that customer preference is influenced by both ratings and popularity.

While some cuisines are rated highly, others dominate in popularity due to higher availability and customer demand. A balanced view of both metrics is essential for better decision-making.
